7. Boat speed: 12 km/h, Current speed: 4 km/h Hard
Let boat speed = b, current speed = c. Downstream: b + c = 48/3 = 16 km/h. Upstream: b - c = 32/4 = 8 km/h. Adding: 2b = 24, so b = 12 km/h. Subtracting: 2c = 8, so c = 4 km/h.

AREA & VOLUME MULTI-STEP (16-22)
16. 25 cubic feet Medium
Base area = 30 sq ft. Height = 16 inches = 16/12 = 4/3 feet. Volume = 30 × 4/3 = 40 cubic feet. When 5/8 full: 40 × 5/8 = 25 cubic feet.
17. About 209.3 minutes Hard
Tank volume = πr²h = π × 4 × 5 = 20π m³. Net inflow rate = 0.5 - 0.2 = 0.3 m³/min. Time = 20π/0.3 = 200π/3 ≈ 209.3 minutes.
18. 1.5 m Medium
Original area = 20 × 15 = 300 m². Let path width = w. Inner rectangle = (20-2w) × (15-2w). Path area = 300 - (20-2w)(15-2w) = 96. Expanding: 300 - (300 - 40w - 30w + 4w²) = 96. 70w - 4w² = 96. 4w² - 70w + 96 = 0. w² - 17.5w + 24 = 0. Using quadratic formula: w = (17.5 ± √(306.25 - 96))/2 = (17.5 ± √210.25)/2 = (17.5 ± 14.5)/2. w = 16 or w = 1.5. Since w must be less than 7.5m (half the width), w = 1.5m.
19. 200π cm³ Very Hard
Cylinder volume = 150π cm³. Cone with same base and height has volume = 1/3 × cylinder volume = 1/3 × 150π = 50π cm³. Combined volume = 150π + 50π = 200π cm³.
20. 16 times Hard
Original radius = r, new radius = 4r. Original area = πr², new area = π(4r)² = 16πr². Area increase factor = 16.
21. 15.625 cm³ Medium
Large cube surface area = 150 cm² = 6a², so a² = 25, a = 5 cm. Small cube edge = 5/2 = 2.5 cm. Small cube volume = (2.5)³ = 15.625 cm³.
22. 600,000 litres Hard
Pool is trapezoidal in cross-section. Average depth = (1 + 3)/2 = 2 m. Volume = length × width × average depth = 25 × 12 × 2 = 600 m³ = 600,000 litres.

32. 1/2 Hard
Total outcomes = 8. At least 2 heads means: exactly 2 heads OR exactly 3 heads. P(exactly 2) = C(3,2)/8 = 3/8. P(exactly 3) = C(3,3)/8 = 1/8. P(at least 2) = 3/8 + 1/8 = 4/8 = 1/2.
33. 5/8 Medium
Even numbers: {2,4,6,8} = 4 numbers. Numbers > 6: {7,8} = 2 numbers. Overlap: {8} = 1 number. P(even OR >6) = P(even) + P(>6) - P(even AND >6) = 4/8 + 2/8 - 1/8 = 5/8.

INTEGER & CALCULATION PROBLEMS (36-40)
36. 5 Medium
Sum of -5 and +7 = 2. Subtracting -3 from 2: 2 - (-3) = 2 + 3 = 5.
37. 19 Medium
Five consecutive numbers with average 17: middle number = 17. Numbers are 15, 16, 17, 18, 19. Largest = 19.
38. 22 Hard
Let numbers be n, n+2, n+4. Sum: n + (n+2) + (n+4) = 3n + 6 = 72. So 3n = 66, n = 22.
39. 12 Medium
Let number = x. 2x + 15 = 3x + 3. 15 - 3 = 3x - 2x. 12 = x.
40. 13 and 15 Hard
Let numbers be n and n+2. n(n+2) = 195. n² + 2n - 195 = 0. Using quadratic formula: n = (-2 ± √(4 + 780))/2 = (-2 ± √784)/2 = (-2 ± 28)/2. n = 13 or n = -15. Since we want positive, n = 13, so numbers are 13 and 15.

Problem-Solving Strategies Required:

  • Setting up equations from word problems
  • Working backwards from given information
  • Multi-step percentage calculations
  • Rate problems (speed, work, flow)
  • Ratio and proportion problems
  • Geometric problem solving

Common Mistake Areas:

  • Not reading problems carefully for all given information
  • Rushing through multi-step calculations
  • Forgetting to convert units (cm to m, minutes to hours, etc.)
  • Setting up incorrect equations for word problems
  • Not checking answers for reasonableness
