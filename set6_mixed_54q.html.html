11+ Mathematics Exam Preparation

Set 6: Percentage Word Problems

Increase, Decrease, Sales, Discounts, Profit & Loss

Test Information:

  • Questions: 50
  • Time Allowed: 40 minutes
  • Total Marks: 50
  • Focus: Real-world percentage applications
  • Show your working clearly
DISCOUNTS & SALES (Questions 1-10)
1. Easy
A jacket originally costs £80. In a sale, it has a 25% discount. What is the sale price?
2. Easy
Sarah buys a book for £12 after getting a 20% discount. What was the original price?
3. Medium
A TV costs £450. It's reduced by 15% in January, then by another 10% in February. What is the final price?
4. Easy
Tom saves £15 on a £60 purchase. What percentage discount did he receive?
5. Medium
A dress is marked down from £85 to £68. What is the percentage discount?
6. Hard
A shop offers 30% off all items. If Lucy pays £91 for a coat, what was its original price?
7. Medium
Electronics are discounted by 18%. If a tablet now costs £328, what was the original price?
8. Easy
A £200 sofa is reduced by £50. What percentage discount is this?
9. Hard
A bicycle costs £240 after a 20% discount, followed by a further £12 discount. What was the original price?
10. Medium
In a closing down sale, all prices are reduced by 35%. Emma buys items totaling £156. How much would she have paid at original prices?
PROFIT & LOSS (Questions 11-20)
11. Easy
A trader buys goods for £120 and sells them for £150. What is his percentage profit?
12. Medium
Maya bought a painting for £800 and sold it for £720. What was her percentage loss?
13. Easy
A shopkeeper makes a 40% profit on cost price. If an item costs him £25, what is his selling price?
14. Medium
James sells a watch for £180, making a 20% profit. What did the watch cost him?
15. Hard
A car dealer buys a car for £8,000 and wants to make a 25% profit. What should be the selling price?
16. Medium
Lisa bought shares for £500 and sold them for £425. What was her percentage loss?
17. Easy
A fruit seller buys apples at £2 per kg and sells at £2.50 per kg. What is his percentage profit?
18. Hard
After selling a laptop for £540, a retailer makes a 35% profit. What was the cost price?
19. Medium
Ben sells his bike for £160, losing 20% of what he paid. What did he originally pay?
20. Hard
A trader marks up his goods by 50% but then gives a 10% discount. What is his actual profit percentage?
INCREASES & DECREASES (Questions 21-30)
21. Easy
The population of a town increases from 25,000 to 28,000. What is the percentage increase?
22. Medium
A company's profits increased by 15% to £230,000. What were the original profits?
23. Easy
After a 12% salary increase, Anna earns £2,240 per month. What was her previous salary?
24. Medium
The price of petrol decreased from £1.40 per litre to £1.26 per litre. What is the percentage decrease?
25. Hard
A school's enrollment drops by 8% to 920 students. How many students were enrolled originally?
26. Easy
House prices in an area increased by 6%. If a house now costs £318,000, what was its previous value?
27. Medium
The number of visitors to a museum increased from 450 to 540 in one year. What was the percentage increase?
28. Hard
After two consecutive 10% increases, a product costs £121. What was the original price?
29. Medium
Membership of a gym decreased by 25% to 600 members. What was the original membership?
30. Hard
A population increases by 4% each year. If it's currently 50,000, what will it be in 2 years?
VAT & TAX (Questions 31-40)
31. Easy
A meal costs £25 plus 20% VAT. What is the total cost?
32. Medium
Including 20% VAT, a laptop costs £600. What is the price before VAT?
33. Easy
A service charge of 12.5% is added to a restaurant bill of £80. What is the total amount?
34. Medium
David pays £84 for a repair, which includes 5% tax. What was the price before tax?
35. Hard
A car costs £18,000 plus 20% VAT and £300 road tax. What is the total cost?
36. Medium
An invoice shows £240 plus 20% VAT. How much VAT is being charged?
37. Easy
A hotel bill is £150 plus 18% service charge. What is the service charge amount?
38. Hard
After paying £360 including 20% VAT, how much VAT did Helen actually pay?
39. Medium
A builder quotes £2,400 plus VAT at 20%. What is the total cost including VAT?
40. Hard
Goods cost £480 including 20% VAT and a 5% handling charge (calculated on the pre-VAT price). What was the original price?
MIXED PERCENTAGE PROBLEMS (Questions 41-50)
41. Medium
In a class of 32 students, 25% wear glasses and 40% of those also wear contact lenses. How many students wear both glasses and contact lenses?
42. Hard
A bank offers 3% annual interest. If £500 is invested, how much will it be worth after 2 years with compound interest?
43. Easy
60% of a football crowd of 45,000 are adults. How many children are in the crowd?
44. Medium
A recipe calls for 250g flour, but Lucy only has 180g. By what percentage should she reduce all other ingredients?
45. Hard
In a sale, prices are reduced by 25%, but then 20% VAT is added to the sale price. A jumper originally cost £40. What does it cost now?
46. Medium
Tom scores 85% in a test worth 60 marks. How many marks did he lose?
47. Easy
In a survey, 35% prefer tea, 45% prefer coffee, and the rest prefer water. If 200 people were surveyed, how many prefer water?
48. Hard
A product's price increased by 20%, then decreased by 15%. If the final price is £102, what was the original price?
49. Medium
Lisa spends 30% of her monthly income on rent, 25% on food, and saves 20%. If she saves £240, what is her monthly income?
50. Hard
A shop owner buys 100 items for £500. He sells 60% at 50% profit and the rest at 20% loss. What is his overall profit or loss percentage?
