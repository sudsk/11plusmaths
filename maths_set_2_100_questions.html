11+ Mathematics Exam Preparation

Set 2: Advanced Problem Solving

More challenging multi-step problems for Year 6/7 students

COMPLEX WORD PROBLEMS (Questions 1-25)
1.
A school has 720 students. 5/8 are girls. Of the girls, 3/5 walk to school and the rest take the bus. How many girls take the bus to school?
2.
Tom saves £15 per week for 12 weeks. He then spends 2/5 of his savings on a bike and 1/3 of the remainder on accessories. How much money does he have left?
3.
A rectangular field is 85m long and 65m wide. A fence costs £12 per metre. What is the total cost to fence the entire perimeter of the field?
4.
In a fruit bowl, the ratio of apples to oranges to bananas is 4:3:5. If there are 36 pieces of fruit in total, and 1/4 of the apples are green, how many red apples are there?
5.
A train travels 285 km in 3 hours 45 minutes, then continues for another 2 hours 15 minutes at the same speed. What is the total distance travelled?
6.
Sarah buys 6 notebooks at £2.85 each, 4 pens at 95p each, and 3 rulers at £1.25 each. She pays with two £20 notes. How much change does she receive?
7.
A water tank is 3/4 full and contains 450 litres. If water flows out at 25 litres per hour and flows in at 18 litres per hour, how long will it take to empty the tank completely?
8.
The temperature at midnight was -5°C. It rose by 3°C every 2 hours until 8 AM, then dropped by 2°C every 3 hours until 2 PM. What was the temperature at 2 PM?
9.
A shopkeeper buys 75 items for £225. He sells 60 items at £4.50 each and the remaining items at £3.75 each. What is his total profit?
10.
Four friends share prize money of £168 in the ratio 3:4:5:6. The person with the largest share spends 2/3 of their money. How much do they have left?
11.
A car uses 9 litres of petrol to travel 108 km. If petrol costs £1.35 per litre, how much would it cost to travel 420 km?
12.
In a test worth 80 marks, Jenny scored 75%. She got 12 questions completely wrong (0 marks each) and the rest were either fully correct (4 marks each) or half correct (2 marks each). How many questions did she get completely correct?
13.
A recipe for 8 people uses 600g flour, 450g sugar, and 320g butter. How much of each ingredient is needed for 14 people?
14.
The sum of four consecutive even numbers is 84. If you multiply the largest number by 3 and subtract 25, what answer do you get?
15.
A square garden has the same area as a rectangular garden measuring 18m by 32m. A path 1.5m wide goes around the outside of the square garden. What is the total area including the path?
16.
Lucy walks to the shops at 4 km/h and takes 45 minutes. She returns home carrying bags at 3 km/h. What is her average speed for the entire journey?
17.
A cinema has 28 rows. The first row has 16 seats, and each subsequent row has 2 more seats than the previous row. How many seats are there in total?
18.
A jacket originally costs £85. In a sale, it's reduced by 25%, then a further 10% is taken off the sale price. What is the final price?
19.
A swimming pool is 25m long, 15m wide, and 2m deep. It takes 6 hours to fill when empty. If the pool is currently 1/4 full, how long will it take to fill completely?
20.
In a school of 450 students, 240 study French, 180 study Spanish, and 90 study both languages. How many students study exactly one of these languages?
21.
A number is divided by 5, then 12 is added, then the result is multiplied by 3 to give 63. What was the original number?
22.
Two machines fill bottles. Machine A fills 150 bottles per hour, Machine B fills 120 bottles per hour. If they work together for 4 hours, then Machine A breaks down and only Machine B works for another 2 hours, how many bottles are filled in total?
23.
The average score of 8 students in a test is 78. If the teacher removes the lowest score (which was 54), what is the new average of the remaining 7 students?
24.
A map has a scale of 1:40,000. Two cities are 12.5 cm apart on the map. A car travels between them at an average speed of 60 km/h. How long does the journey take?
25.
Ahmed has 60% more marbles than Ben. Charlie has 25% fewer marbles than Ahmed. If Ben has 40 marbles, how many marbles do all three boys have together?
ADVANCED NUMBER PATTERNS & ALGEBRA (Questions 26-50)
26.

27.
If 3x - 7 = 14, what is the value of 5x + 9?
28.

30.
Find the pattern: 2, 8, 18, 32, 50, ?, ?
31.
If a = 2b + 5 and b = 3c - 4, express a in terms of c.
32.
The sum of the first n triangular numbers is n(n+1)(n+2)/6. Find the sum of the first 5 triangular numbers.
33.
Simplify: 7x + 3y - 4x + 8y - 2x + y
34.
What is the 12th term in the sequence where each term is the sum of the previous two terms, starting with 3, 7?
35.
If x + y = 12 and x - y = 4, find the values of x and y.
36.
Find the missing numbers: 1, 8, 27, ?, 125, ?, 343, ?
37.
Expand and simplify: 3(2x - 4) + 5(x + 3)
38.
The sequence 5, 12, 21, 32, 45, ... follows what rule?
39.
If 2x/3 + 4 = 10, find the value of x.
40.
Find the 15th term of the arithmetic sequence: 7, 12, 17, 22, ...
41.
What comes next: 3, 4, 7, 11, 18, 29, ?
42.
Solve: 5(x - 2) - 3(x + 1) = 7
43.
Find the sum of all multiples of 7 between 20 and 100.
44.
If f(x) = 3x - 5, find f(8) - f(3).
45.
The sequence starts: 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, ... Which term equals 169?
46.
A cylindrical water tank has radius 1.2m and height 2.5m. If it's filled to 80% capacity, how many litres of water does it contain? (Use π = 3.14, 1 cubic metre = 1000 litres)
47.
A father is currently 4 times as old as his son. In 12 years, he will be twice as old as his son will be then. How old is the father now?
48.
A charity raises £3600. They give 1/4 to a hospital, 1/3 of the remainder to a school, and 2/5 of what's left to an animal shelter. How much money do they still have to distribute?
49.
A train 180m long passes a platform 420m long in 24 seconds. What is the speed of the train in km/h?
50.
A right-angled triangle has legs of 15cm and 20cm. Another similar triangle has a hypotenuse of 40cm. What is the area of the larger triangle?

74.

75.

PROBABILITY, STATISTICS & DATA (Questions 76-100)
76.
A bag contains 8 red balls, 5 blue balls, and 7 green balls. Two balls are drawn without replacement. What is the probability that both balls are red?
77.
The test scores of 12 students are: 68, 72, 75, 78, 82, 84, 85, 88, 90, 92, 95, 98. Find the median and the range.
78.
A fair six-sided die is rolled three times. What is the probability of getting at least one six?
79.
In a survey of 200 people: 120 like chocolate, 80 like vanilla, 60 like strawberry, 30 like chocolate and vanilla, 25 like chocolate and strawberry, 20 like vanilla and strawberry, and 10 like all three flavors. How many people like none of these flavors?
80.

84.

85.

86.
