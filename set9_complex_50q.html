11+ Mathematics Exam Preparation

Set 9: Complex Multi-Step Problems

Advanced problem-solving with multiple operations and logical reasoning

Test Information:

  • Questions: 50
  • Time Allowed: 50 minutes
  • Total Marks: 50
  • Multi-step problems requiring advanced reasoning
  • Show ALL working clearly for full marks
  • Difficulty: Medium (20), Hard (20), Very Hard (10)
MULTI-STEP DISTANCE, SPEED & TIME (Questions 1-8)
1. Medium
Sarah cycles from home to the library at 12 km/h, spends 30 minutes there, then cycles back home at 8 km/h. If her total journey time is 2 hours 30 minutes, how far is the library from her home?
2. Hard
A train travels 180 km at 60 km/h, then stops for 20 minutes, then travels 240 km at 80 km/h. Meanwhile, a car starts 1 hour later and travels the same total distance at constant speed, arriving at the same time as the train. What is the car's speed?
3. Medium
Tom walks to school at 4 km/h and arrives 5 minutes late. If he had walked at 5 km/h, he would have arrived 4 minutes early. How far is his school from home?
4. Very Hard
Two cyclists start from opposite ends of a 120 km track. The faster cyclist travels at 25 km/h and the slower at 15 km/h. After they meet, each continues to the other's starting point, then immediately turns around. Where will they meet for the second time?
5. Hard
A delivery van travels 90 km at 45 km/h, then gets stuck in traffic for 30 minutes, then travels 60 km at 30 km/h. If the van needs to maintain an average speed of 36 km/h for the entire journey, how fast must it travel the remaining 75 km?
6. Medium
Lisa runs the first half of a race at 8 km/h and the second half at 12 km/h. If the race is 10 km long, what is her average speed for the entire race?
7. Hard
A boat travels 48 km downstream in 3 hours, then 32 km upstream in 4 hours. Find the speed of the boat in still water and the speed of the current.
8. Very Hard
Two trains of lengths 150 m and 200 m are travelling towards each other at speeds of 54 km/h and 72 km/h respectively. How long will it take for them to completely cross each other?
COIN & MONEY PROBLEMS (Questions 9-15)
9. Medium
Emma has 15 coins worth £2.10 in total, made up of 10p and 20p coins only. How many of each type does she have?
10. Hard
A collection of 50p and £1 coins has a total value of £47. If there are 8 more 50p coins than £1 coins, how many of each coin are there?
11. Medium
James has twice as many 5p coins as 2p coins, and the total value is 64p. How many coins of each type does he have?
12. Hard
A cashier has £45 in £5 and £10 notes. If she has 3 fewer £10 notes than £5 notes, and then receives 2 more £5 notes and 1 more £10 note, what is the new total value?
13. Very Hard
In a charity collection box, there are 1p, 2p, and 5p coins. The number of 2p coins is twice the number of 1p coins, and there are 5 fewer 5p coins than 1p coins. If the total value is £1.83, how many coins of each type are there?
14. Medium
Sophie has 20 coins worth £4.40, consisting of 20p and 50p coins only. After spending 3 of the 50p coins, what is the value of her remaining coins?
15. Hard
A parking meter contains 20p, 50p and £1 coins. There are equal numbers of 20p and £1 coins, and twice as many 50p coins as 20p coins. If the total value is £18, how many coins are there in total?
AREA & VOLUME MULTI-STEP (Questions 16-22)
16. Medium
The area of the base of a rectangular tank measures 30 sq ft, and its height is 16 inches. Find the number of cubic feet of water in the tank when it is 5/8 full.
17. Hard
A cylindrical water tank has radius 2 m and height 5 m. If water is pumped in at a rate of 0.5 m³ per minute, and simultaneously pumped out at 0.2 m³ per minute, how long will it take to fill the empty tank?
18. Medium
A rectangular garden is 20 m by 15 m. A path of uniform width runs around the inside perimeter. If the path has an area of 96 m², what is its width?
19. Very Hard
A cone and cylinder have the same base radius and height. If the volume of the cylinder is 150π cm³, what is the total volume when both shapes are combined?
20. Hard
The radius of a circle is increased by three times (so the new radius is 4 times the original). By how many times is the area increased?
21. Medium
A cube has surface area 150 cm². A smaller cube has edge length half that of the larger cube. What is the volume of the smaller cube?
22. Hard
A swimming pool is 25 m long, 12 m wide, with depths of 1 m at the shallow end and 3 m at the deep end (linear slope). How many litres of water does it hold when full?
DISCOUNTS, PERCENTAGES & FRACTIONS (Questions 23-29)
23. Medium
A shirt is marked down 25%, then an additional 10% is taken off the sale price. If the final price is £27, what was the original price?
24. Hard
In a sale, all items are reduced by 30%. Sarah pays £126 for a jacket, then gets an additional 5% staff discount on the sale price. What would the jacket have cost at the original price without any discounts?
25. Medium
Tom spends 2/5 of his money on books, then 1/3 of what remains on food. If he has £18 left, how much money did he start with?
26. Very Hard
A product's price increased by 20%, then decreased by 15%, then increased again by 10%. If the final price is £112.20, what was the original price?
27. Hard
Lisa has £150. She spends 40% on clothes, then 25% of what remains on books, then 1/6 of what's left on food. How much money does she have left?
28. Medium
A store offers 'buy 2 get 1 free' on items costing £15 each. If Emma wants 11 items, what is the cheapest amount she can pay?
29. Hard
A company's profits increased by 25% in the first year, decreased by 20% in the second year, then increased by 15% in the third year. If the final profit is £115,000, what was the original profit?
PROBABILITY PROBLEMS (Questions 30-35)
30. Medium
What is the probability of randomly selecting 'A' from group (A,B) and 'C' from group (C,D,E)?
31. Medium
A bag contains 5 red balls, 3 blue balls, and 7 yellow balls. If two balls are drawn without replacement, what is the probability both are red?
32. Hard
Three coins are tossed simultaneously. What is the probability of getting at least two heads?
33. Medium
A spinner has 8 equal sections numbered 1-8. What is the probability of spinning an even number or a number greater than 6?
34. Hard
From a standard deck of 52 cards, what is the probability of drawing a red card, then without replacement, drawing a black card?
35. Very Hard
Box A contains 4 red and 6 blue balls. Box B contains 3 red and 7 blue balls. A ball is randomly chosen from each box. What is the probability that both balls are the same colour?
INTEGER & CALCULATION PROBLEMS (Questions 36-40)
36. Medium
When -3 is subtracted from the sum of -5 and +7, what is the result?
37. Medium
If the average of five consecutive numbers is 17, what is the largest number?
38. Hard
The sum of three consecutive even numbers is 72. What is the smallest of these numbers?
39. Medium
When twice a number is increased by 15, the result is 3 more than three times the number. Find the number.
40. Hard
The product of two consecutive odd numbers is 195. What are the two numbers?
COMPLEX WORD PROBLEMS (Questions 41-50)
41. Hard
At Marlborough High, three out of every four graduates go on to college. Of these, two out of every three graduate from college. What percent of students graduating from Marlborough High will graduate from college?
42. Very Hard
A purple dye is made by mixing red and blue dye in a ratio of 3:4. An orange dye is made by mixing red dye and yellow dye in a ratio of 3:2. If equal amounts of purple and orange dye are mixed, what fraction of the new mixture is red dye?
43. Hard
Harrow County has a population of 4000 and requires 150 litres of water per head per day. The county has a tank measuring 20m × 15m × 6m. For how many days will the water in the tank last?
44. Hard
A secondary football team plans to play 25 matches. If the football team wins 60% of the first 10 matches, how many more matches must the team win to finish the season winning 80% of all planned matches?
45. Very Hard
A small clothing store sells three different types of accessories. 20% are scarves, 60% are ties, and the other 40 accessories are belts. If half of the ties are replaced with scarves, how many scarves will the store have?
46. Medium
A recipe for 6 people requires 400g flour, 250ml milk, and 3 eggs. If Sarah wants to cook for 9 people but only has 2 eggs, how much flour should she use?
47. Hard
In a school election, candidate A received 45% of votes, candidate B received 35%, and the remaining 120 votes went to candidate C. How many students voted in total?
48. Very Hard
A factory produces widgets in three sizes: small (30%), medium (50%), and large (20%). If defect rates are 2% for small, 3% for medium, and 5% for large widgets, what percentage of all widgets produced are defective?
49. Hard
Two taps can fill a swimming pool. Tap A alone takes 6 hours, tap B alone takes 4 hours. If both taps are opened together, but there's also a drain that empties the pool in 12 hours, how long will it take to fill the empty pool?
50. Very Hard
A train leaves London at 2:30 PM travelling at 120 km/h. Another train leaves Birmingham (180 km away) at 3:15 PM travelling towards London at 100 km/h. At what time do they meet, and how far from London?
